# Bucket Examples - Real-World Scenarios

## Overview
This reference provides concrete examples of bucket configurations for different life situations, helping users understand how to structure their own bucket systems.

## Example 1: Freelancer (Variable Income)

### Income Assumptions
- Average monthly: R50,000 (varies R30,000 - R80,000)
- Quarterly tax obligations
- No employer benefits

### Bucket Structure
```
💰 Income Flow (R50,000)
├── 🏦 Pre-cuts (32.5%)
│   ├── Tax Provision (27.5%): R13,750
│   └── Pay Yourself First (5%): R2,500
│
├── 🏠 Essentials (30% of remainder): R10,125
│   ├── Rent: R8,000 (fixed)
│   ├── Utilities: R800 (fixed)
│   ├── Insurance: R1,325 (fixed)
│   └── Overflow → Emergency Fund
│
├── 🛡️ Protection (20%): R6,750
│   ├── Emergency Fund: R4,050 (until R150k)
│   ├── Medical Reserve: R1,350
│   └── Equipment Fund: R1,350
│
├── 💳 Debt (10%): R3,375
│   └── Credit Card: R3,375 (18% interest)
│
├── 🍽️ Living (25%): R8,437
│   ├── Food: R4,000
│   ├── Transport: R2,000
│   ├── Personal: R1,437
│   └── Subscriptions: R1,000
│
├── 🎯 Goals (10%): R3,375
│   ├── New Laptop: R1,687
│   ├── Vacation: R1,688
│   └── Overflow → Investment
│
└── 🎮 Play (5%): R1,688
    └── Must spend monthly!
```

### Special Rules
1. **Income Smoothing**: When income > R50k, excess → Buffer bucket
2. **Dry Month Protection**: If income < R40k, draw from Buffer first
3. **Tax Quarter**: Month before tax due, double Tax Provision percentage

## Example 2: Young Family (Dual Income)

### Income Assumptions
- Combined monthly: R75,000
- Both permanently employed
- Two young children

### Bucket Structure
```
💰 Combined Income (R75,000)
├── 🏦 Pre-cuts (15%)
│   ├── Retirement (10%): R7,500
│   └── Education Fund (5%): R3,750
│
├── 🏠 Fixed Expenses (45%): R28,688
│   ├── Bond Payment: R15,000
│   ├── Insurance Bundle: R3,500
│   ├── School Fees: R6,000
│   ├── Car Payment: R4,188
│   └── Overflow → Emergency
│
├── 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Family Living (25%): R15,937
│   ├── Groceries: R8,000
│   ├── Fuel: R3,000
│   ├── Kids Activities: R2,000
│   ├── Household: R2,937
│   └── Overflow → Family Fun
│
├── 🛡️ Safety Net (10%): R6,375
│   ├── Emergency (6 months): R4,000
│   ├── Medical Gap: R1,375
│   └── Home Maintenance: R1,000
│
└── 💫 Dreams & Fun (5%): R3,188
    ├── Annual Holiday: R2,000
    ├── Date Nights: R1,188
    └── Overflow → Kids Future
```

### Family Rules
1. **Both Parents Rule**: Both salaries must arrive before distribution
2. **School Priority**: School fees filled first, always
3. **Emergency Override**: If Emergency < 3 months expenses, +10% allocation

## Example 3: Debt Destroyer Mode

### Situation
- Monthly income: R40,000
- Total debt: R120,000
- Goal: Debt free in 18 months

### Aggressive Bucket Structure
```
💰 Income (R40,000)
├── 🏦 Survival Mode (60%)
│   ├── Rent: R6,000
│   ├── Food (Basic): R3,000
│   ├── Transport: R1,500
│   ├── Utilities: R800
│   ├── Phone: R300
│   └── Emergency Min: R2,400
│
├── 💣 Debt Destruction (35%): R14,000
│   ├── Credit Card 1 (24%): R5,000 → R0
│   ├── Credit Card 2 (22%): R4,000 → R0  
│   ├── Personal Loan (14%): R3,000
│   └── Store Credit (21%): R2,000
│
└── 🧠 Sanity (5%): R2,000
    ├── Basic Play: R1,000
    └── Micro Emergency: R1,000
```

### Debt Avalanche Rules
1. **Highest Interest First**: Always pay minimums, excess to highest rate
2. **Snowball Option**: When Card 1 paid → add payment to Card 2
3. **Celebration Milestone**: Each debt cleared → R500 bonus to Play
4. **No New Debt**: Locked rule, cannot override

## Example 4: Business Owner

### Assumptions
- Business income: Variable R80,000-120,000
- Personal salary: R50,000
- Separating business/personal

### Dual Structure
```
🏢 BUSINESS ACCOUNT
├── Operating (40%)
├── Tax Reserve (30%)
├── Growth Fund (20%)
└── Owner Draw (10%)

👤 PERSONAL (From R50k Salary)
├── Pre-cuts (25%)
│   ├── Personal Tax (15%)
│   └── Investments (10%)
├── Living (50%)
│   ├── All personal expenses
│   └── Lifestyle choices
├── Savings (20%)
│   └── Personal goals
└── Play (5%)
```

### Business Rules
1. **Salary First**: Personal salary before owner draws
2. **Tax Sacred**: Business tax never touched
3. **Reinvestment**: If growth opportunity → pause owner draws

## Example 5: FIRE Seeker (Financial Independence)

### Aggressive Savings Focus
- Income: R60,000
- Goal: 70% savings rate
- Minimal lifestyle

### Extreme Bucket Structure
```
💰 Income (R60,000)
├── 🔥 FIRE Fund (70%): R42,000
│   ├── Index Funds (40%): R24,000
│   ├── Emergency (10%): R6,000
│   ├── Property Fund (20%): R12,000
│   └── Overflow → Crypto (risky)
│
├── 🏠 Bare Essentials (25%): R15,000
│   ├── Shared Rent: R4,000
│   ├── Groceries (meal prep): R2,000
│   ├── Transport (bike): R500
│   ├── Utilities Share: R500
│   └── Everything else: R8,000
│
└── 🎯 Strategic (5%): R3,000
    ├── Skill Development: R2,000
    └── Minimal Play: R1,000
```

### FIRE Rules
1. **Savings Sacred**: Cannot reduce FIRE % for any reason
2. **Lifestyle Creep Block**: Raises go 100% to FIRE
3. **Side Hustle Rule**: All extra income → FIRE Fund

## Common Patterns

### Starter Template (Any Income)
```
Income → 
├── Tax/Savings (20-30%)
├── Fixed Costs (40-50%)
├── Variable Living (20-30%)
├── Goals (5-10%)
└── Play (5%)
```

### Priority Order
1. **Pre-cuts**: Tax, Pay Yourself First
2. **Survival**: Rent, Food, Transport
3. **Protection**: Emergency, Insurance
4. **Obligations**: Debts, Commitments
5. **Growth**: Savings, Goals
6. **Life**: Play, Experiences

### Universal Rules
1. **Emergency First**: Until 3-6 months expenses
2. **High Interest Debt**: Priority over savings
3. **Play Required**: Mental health essential
4. **Review Monthly**: Adjust as needed
